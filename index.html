Leverage cuts both ways

If Thunder falls below 15% the loan can be liquidated. More size means a thinner margin for being wrong.

The hard part: near-resolution pricing

Close to resolution, Thunder snaps toward 0% or 100% and liquidity thins. Pricing that collateral safely is what we're still solving.

The trader never sees a lending market, a loan, or a health factor. They pick a side, drag a slider, and buy. That native experience is the product the prediction market sells — not a raw pool a user has to operate themselves.
What the user sees vs. what runs

User-facing: boost slider, "max win," "most you can lose," auto-protection. Hidden: the collateralized borrow, LTV, liquidation engine, and the pre-close unwind.

Still being solved

Aggregated curated liquidity across markets, curation to exclude open-ended-resolution events, and the near-resolution pricing oracle. The borrow layer is the part the prediction market never has to build.
